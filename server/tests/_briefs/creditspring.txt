Campaign overview:

Home Alone, the quintessential Christmas movie, turns 35 this December. But for many families, Christmas at home is far from picture-perfect.

With rising costs of living and 'Chris-flation' biting harder each year, people fall into debt just to afford the basics - from decorations to plastic trees to hosting essentials. We propose a festive pop-up shop lending out Christmas must-haves in the run up to the festive period (November), helping families create magical moments at home without the financial strain.

This activation delivers rich human stories, visually playful moments, and a powerful platform to talk about the real cost of Christmas and how borrowing better can make the season fairer.

We takeover a shop – call it Home a Loan – and kit it out really visually. The items will correlate back to the research findings (TBC on what these are).

Location: somewhere central, likely Soho. We're already sourcing a blank space for the pop-up and have a separate location site fee for this. Will be small to mid-sized.

Dates: Launch the shop opening on Wednesday 19th November. (dressing on the 18th, de-rig on the 20th).
